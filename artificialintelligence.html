AI Frequently Asked Questions

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that aims to create systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and language translation.

AI is the broad concept of machines being able to act intelligently. Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI that allows systems to learn from data, identify patterns, and improve automatically.

Generative AI is capable of generating text, images, music, or code based on prompts. Examples include ChatGPT and Midjourney.

AI learns through training. Large datasets are processed to discover patterns, allowing the system to make predictions or generate new content.

AI can automate repetitive tasks but also creates new job opportunities and enhances human productivity instead of fully replacing people.

Hallucinations occur when an AI generates answers that appear confident and logical but are factually incorrect or meaningless.

AI carries risks such as bias, privacy concerns, and misuse, but ethical guidelines and regulations are being developed to ensure safe usage.

A prompt is an instruction given to an AI model. The quality of the output depends heavily on how clear and detailed the prompt is.

AI Glossary

Neural Network

A system inspired by the human brain, consisting of connected nodes that process and learn from data.

Dataset

A collection of structured or unstructured data used to train AI models.

Algorithm

A set of instructions that an AI system follows to solve problems or make decisions.

Deep Learning

A subset of Machine Learning that uses deep neural networks to model complex patterns.
