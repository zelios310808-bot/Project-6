AI Frequently Asked Questions

Click on each question to explore core concepts of Artificial Intelligence.

What is Artificial Intelligence (AI)? ▼

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science focused on creating systems that can perform tasks requiring human intelligence, such as perception, reasoning, learning, and language understanding.

What is the difference between AI and Machine Learning? ▼

AI is the broad concept of intelligent machines. Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI that enables systems to learn from data, detect patterns, and improve performance without being explicitly programmed.

What is Generative AI? ▼

Generative AI refers to AI systems capable of creating new content such as text, images, music, or code. Examples include ChatGPT for text and Midjourney for image generation.

How does AI learn? ▼

AI learns through training. Large datasets are fed into algorithms, allowing the system to identify patterns and relationships, which are then used to make predictions or generate outputs.

Can AI replace human jobs? ▼

AI can automate repetitive tasks and may replace some jobs, but it also creates new roles and enhances human productivity by acting as a supportive tool rather than a full replacement.

What are "Hallucinations" in AI? ▼

Hallucinations occur when an AI system generates responses that sound confident but are factually incorrect or meaningless, often due to limitations in training data or reasoning.

Is AI dangerous? ▼

AI has risks such as bias, privacy issues, and misuse. Responsible development, ethical guidelines, and regulations are essential to ensure AI benefits society safely.

What is a Prompt? ▼

A prompt is an instruction or input given to an AI system. The clarity and structure of a prompt greatly influence the quality of the AI’s response.
