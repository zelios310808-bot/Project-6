AI Frequently Asked Questions

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science focused on creating systems that can perform tasks requiring human intelligence such as perception, reasoning, learning, language understanding and decision-making.

AI is the broad concept of machines acting intelligently. Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI that allows systems to learn from data, identify patterns, and improve automatically without being explicitly programmed.

Generative AI refers to AI systems that can create new content such as text, images, audio, or video. Examples include ChatGPT for text generation and Midjourney for image creation.

AI learns through a process called training, where large datasets are used to adjust model parameters. Over time, the system improves its predictions or outputs by minimizing errors.

AI can automate repetitive tasks, which may replace some jobs. However, it also creates new roles and enhances human productivity rather than fully replacing humans.

Hallucinations occur when AI systems generate responses that sound confident but are factually incorrect or nonsensical, often due to limitations in training data.

AI can pose risks such as bias, privacy issues, and misuse (e.g., deepfakes). Responsible development, ethical guidelines, and regulations help mitigate these risks.

A prompt is the input or instruction given to an AI system to guide its output. Well-crafted prompts can significantly improve the quality of AI-generated results.

AI Glossary

Click on each term to reveal its definition

Machine Learning

Systems that automatically learn patterns from data without explicit programming.

Neural Network

Models inspired by the human brain, consisting of interconnected nodes (neurons).

Deep Learning

A subset of Machine Learning using multi-layer neural networks.

Large Language Model (LLM)

AI models trained on massive text datasets to understand and generate language.

Algorithm

A set of rules or instructions that guide AI decision-making.

Training Data

Data used to teach AI systems how to perform tasks.

Prompt Engineering

The practice of designing effective prompts to improve AI output quality.

Bias

Systematic errors in AI outputs caused by imbalanced or flawed training data.
